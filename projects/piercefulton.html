Pierce Fulton

Branding/Identity

Challenge:

Pierce Fulton is a rising star in the very competitive electronic dance music industry. His unique blend of genres and instruments earned him some hit tracks over the past few years. Fans became accustomed to his unique sound, but although his music grew, his brand remained static. He needed to strengthen his identity in order to support his great music, and to compete with the biggest names in the business. At the time of this project (April 2015), his branding was undeveloped. It consisted of a logo that lived across his social media channels (Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter), and a bit of album art. The challenge was create a unique identity that would unify his brand in order to position him as a major player in the industry.


Response:

After conducting research based on the target audience, competition, and Pierce himself, I concluded that the best approach would be to focus on his crazy and quirky personality, which is also reflected in his music. That’s what makes him special, and his audience relates with him in that regard. Based on this premise, I decided to keep his existing logo and use some of his album art as inspiration to build the rest of the brand around it.

The green theme came about to represent his zany nature. It’s a bit edgy and dangerous, almost like an element of poison or toxic waste. It also represents the idea of Pierce’s music spreading to all his listeners and everything he touches — once it touches you, you become hooked. This concept is enforced through the dot in the parallax scrolling splash page and the promotional video, which turns everything to green as the green dot passes. His personality was portrayed further through an organic iconographic set which would be used as a system throughout his channels. The icons served as symbols to represent the various touch points users would experience with Pierce.


Outcome:

Due to the hypothetical nature of the project, it is impossible to measure the effectiveness of in a real world scenario. Instead, I determined success based on user testing. The criteria consisted of: overall unity, representation of Pierce, connection with the audience, and differentiation. The feedback was very positive, and based on the criteria, the project was successful.
