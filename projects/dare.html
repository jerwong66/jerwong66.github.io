Challenge:

Dare Creative is a hypothetical brand that offers creative goods such as pencils, pens, notebooks, and markers. Dare was built around the idea of creative exploration. The products we offer are the starting point to discovering great ideas, a concept in itself that is incredibly powerful. Our primary target audience is young creatives. They value the culture and story behind the products they purchase. The challenge for this project was to position Dare as the go-to creative goods brand in order to capture this audience and turn them into lifelong customers.

The primary goal of the package was to differentiate the products with the branding, and to create a strong shelf presence over the competition.


Response:

The name Dare came from the idea of challenge, and the ability to challenge your creative process. We want to inspire our audience to explore the boundaries of creative freedom with the help of our products. Dare is an expression of freedom as a brand, but also for our customers. Copy lines for an ad campaign would have a challenge to the audience, followed by “Dare to be you.” For example: “Dare to explore. Dare to be you” or “Dare to be wrong. Dare to be you.”

Key elements of Dare’s brand character are bold, radical, imaginative, adventurous, and expressive. The mark evokes movement and tension. It is a balance between structure and edgy, with a bold presence that supports the statement it represents.

The ink clouds are used as an abstract symbol to represent the depth and the mystery of the creative process. Our products are the vehicles which help users navigate through the unknown, just like a ship in the sea.

The display stand is packed tightly to improve the structural rigidity while the products are on display, but also during shipping, and to minimize waste. The staircase was added to give the display a strong shelf presence, and to integrate the wooden pencil stand included in the package. The pencil stand would be a wooden version of the staircase that would sit upside-down on top of the boxes during shipping, creating a squared package. The pencil stand is intended to be used alongside the package display so the seller can feature the products themselves, allowing for individual sale and product testing.


Outcome:

As I was working on this project, I began to embody the Dare philosophies. I started challenging my own creative process, and thinking about things differently which led to the discovery of new possibilities. It was a rewarding process which has inspired me to continue the path of discovery.
