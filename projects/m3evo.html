BMW M3 Evolution

Information Design

Client: Student Work

Scope: Image Development, Illustration, Semiotics

Challenge:

The BMW M3 is an iconic car in the automotive world. It started back in 1986 when the M Division created the legendary E30 M3 that won championships across the world. Fast forward 30 years and 4 generations later, we have the latest interpretation of the M3.

In honour of this great achievement, I've decided to explore each generation of the M3 — taking the audience on a journey of what made each car truly special.

The challenge was to represent the fundamental characteristics of each generation in order to tell the story of the M3’s evolution in a universal matter with minimal words.


Response:

I started by researching the trademark character of the M3 to get a better understanding of the car. Key characteristics that made the M3 great were its chassis balance, precise control, and the metallic rasp of an inline 6 cylinder engine. Based on this information, I created a set of icons that encapsulate the driving dynamics of each vehicle, which then became a cohesive system between each generation. The concept is that the reader should be able to gain a general understanding of each car’s personality by looking at the icons. Some of the challenges I came across were that certain aspects were more difficult to represent, such as the “metallic rasp” of the engine note, or the “bland burble” of the new turbocharged engine. Due to the abstract nature of some of these characteristics, it was difficult to create unique icons without unintended meanings being associated with them.

Other key elements of the cars include the body profile, fascia, and engines which serve to represent the vehicle’s physical changes. The size of the cars play an important role in the M3’s story as well. Traditionally, BMW’s were known to be small and light, but overtime, their cars evolved and became larger and heavier. As such, outlines of each generation were underlaid for easy comparison to reflect this aspect of the story.

The primary target audience are car enthusiasts and potential sports car buyers. The infographic serves as an educational tool (online and in print) explaining the driving dynamics of the cars, and can be used to promote the M3 brand. The infographic also serves as a nice poster on the wall for car fans that appreciate the history of the legendary M3.


Outcome:

This project was a very interesting to work on. It had its own unique challenges that required a lot of user testing to figure out what was working and what wasn’t. In the end, it was a valuable experience, and a good lesson in problem solving and compromise.

To determine the success of the concept, user testing was conducted with many people that were not in the target audience (non-car people). Most users were able to understand the basic character of the cars, but some icons were more effective than others.
